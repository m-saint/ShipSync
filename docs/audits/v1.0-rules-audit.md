# Rules-Fidelity Audit — ShipSync v1.0 (closed in v1.0.2)

**Scope:** Cross-reference every ShipSync surface that encodes a game rule against the *Aetherial Expanse Core Book* (the rulebook checked in at the workspace root). UX fidelity is tracked separately in [`v1.0-ux-audit.md`](./v1.0-ux-audit.md); this doc is purely about whether the numbers, defaults, vocabulary, and derivations on screen match the rulebook on the table.
**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Source of truth:** `Aetherial-Expanse-Core-Book.pdf`. Page citations below refer to the printed page numbers in that PDF, not the file-page index.

ShipSync deliberately doesn't *adjudicate* the rules — no dice rolls, no automatic phase advancement, no contested checks. But it does encode rule **defaults**, **vocabulary**, and **derived displays** (Mettle baseline, skeleton crew, supply caps, conditions, pursuit gap). When any of those drift from the rulebook, the captain at the table either has to do mental math to reconcile or — worse — runs the wrong number for a session before noticing. This audit catches that drift before it ships.

## Findings

### CRITICAL

#### C1: `DEFAULT_HP_MAX_BY_SIZE` was wrong at every size

**Before (v1.0.1):** `tiny: 5, small: 10, medium: 20, large: 35, huge: 60, gargantuan: 100`. Stepper increment was 5.
**After (v1.0.2):** `tiny: 9, small: 12, medium: 15, large: 20, huge: 25, gargantuan: 30`. Stepper increment is 1.

PDF source: page 169, Ship Hit Points and Mobility table. Pre-v1.0.2 values were 2-3x the canonical seeds — chartering a Frigate landed on 35 HP instead of the rulebook's 20, which would silently inflate every battle past the third round. The `step={5}` on `ProfileSection`'s Hull (max) stepper made it impossible to land on the canonical odd values without manual typing. ✅ Closed at `rules.js:189`–`:196`, `ProfileSection.svelte` (step + max), `AddShipDialog.svelte` (step + max + helpText).

#### C2: `DEFAULT_EXPLOSION_DC_BY_SIZE` was 4 too low at every size

**Before (v1.0.1):** `tiny: 8, small: 10, medium: 12, large: 14, huge: 16, gargantuan: 18`.
**After (v1.0.2):** `tiny: 12, small: 14, medium: 16, large: 18, huge: 20, gargantuan: 22`.

PDF source: page 189, Explosion DCs table. Pre-v1.0.2 values made every ship roughly 20% more likely to explode out from under the captain than the rulebook says. ✅ Closed at `rules.js:220`–`:227`.

#### C3: Crew-by-size defaults were cargo-cult numbers; skeleton mark used the wrong ratio

**Before (v1.0.1):** crew defaults were `tiny: 6, small: 12, medium: 24, large: 48, huge: 96, gargantuan: 200`; skeleton mark = `floor(max / 4)`.
**After (v1.0.2):** crew defaults match the smallest canonical type at each size (`tiny: 8, small: 24, medium: 40, large: 64, huge: 99, gargantuan: 120`); skeleton mark = `floor(max / 2)`. Selecting a canonical Type in the charter form now seeds a type-exact crew/skeleton pair from the page-198 table.

PDF source: page 198, Officers & Crew + Short-Staffed (skeleton at half max). The `/4` ratio meant a Sloop hit "skeleton" at 6 instead of 12, suppressing the Short-Staffed warning until the crew was already non-functional. ✅ Closed at `rules.js:242`–`:249`, `derivations.js` (`skeletonCrewFor`, `makeShip` integration), `AddShipDialog.svelte` (type-aware seed), `CombatReadinessSection.svelte` (hint copy).

### HIGH

#### H1: `baselineMettle` was summing reputation across every flag in the locker

**Before (v1.0.1):** `baselineMettle(ship) = MIN + captainRank + sum(reputation across every flag in flown + known)`.
**After (v1.0.2):** Only the **currently flying** flag's total reputation contributes. Faction flags contribute zero (a faction's reputation belongs to the faction, not the wearer of its colors).

PDF source: page 199 — Beginning Mettle uses the *flag the ship is presently flying*. The pre-v1.0.2 calc could overstate Mettle by 5+ on a multi-flag ship. ✅ Closed at `derivations.js#baselineMettle`, `derivations.test.js` (new fixtures).

#### H2: METTLE_HINT described a "drift" rule that doesn't exist

**Before (v1.0.1):** Hint said "Mettle drifts toward the baseline at the start of each round." There is no such mechanic in the rulebook — Mettle is a discretionary spend that resets only on Stricken / shore leave / scene end (depending on the table's house calls).
**After (v1.0.2):** Hint describes the page-199 baseline derivation literally and stops at "spend it down during Status, refill at shore leave or after key events."

✅ Closed at `CombatReadinessSection.svelte` and `ShipDetail.svelte` METTLE_HINT.

#### H3: Reputation was a single integer; rulebook tracks four axes

**Before (v1.0.1):** `Flag.reputation` was a single signed integer. Conflict resolution sorted by that integer. Mettle counted it directly.
**After (v1.0.2):** `Flag.reputation` is a four-axis tally `{ good, evil, lawful, chaotic }` (PDF p. 200, Reputation Types). Total reputation is the sum across all four; that's what feeds Mettle and conflict resolution sorts. Legacy single-integer save files are migrated to `{ good: <n>, evil: 0, lawful: 0, chaotic: 0 }` on load (`loadFile.js#normalizeFlagState`). The `setShipFlag` mutator still accepts a number for backward compat in JSON imports / scripts, mapping it to the good axis with a deprecation-friendly path.

✅ Closed at `types.js` (new `ReputationAxis`, `Reputation`), `rules.js` (`REPUTATION_AXES`, labels), `derivations.js` (`makeReputation`, `normalizeReputation`, `flagTotalReputation`, `reputationsEqual`), `workspace.svelte.js` mutators, `loadFile.js` migration, `flagConflicts.js` resolver, `FlagCard.svelte` UI (four steppers), `FlagsSection.svelte` (deep-copy on reconcile, total-rep display in conflict banner), `ShipPrintSheet.svelte` (total-rep + faction marker), plus matching test updates.

### MEDIUM

#### M1: False-flag hint copy implied a mechanical effect that doesn't exist

**Before (v1.0.1):** "Flying false colors. Reputation effects are inverted."
**After (v1.0.2):** "Flying these colors deceptively. The rulebook treats false colors as narrative — your captain shouldn't earn reputation under them. Mettle still draws from this flag's tally if it's the one flying."

PDF source: page 200 — false flags are a deception fiction, not a sign-flip on reputation gain. ✅ Closed at `FlagCard.svelte`.

#### M2: Mobility didn't follow size in `makeShip`

**Before (v1.0.1):** `makeShip` always seeded mobility to `'balanced'`.
**After (v1.0.2):** Seeds from `DEFAULT_MOBILITY_BY_SIZE` (Tiny/Small → high, Medium/Large → balanced, Huge/Gargantuan → low) per page-169 table, with explicit override allowed. The charter form tracks a `mobilityIsCustom` flag so re-changing size won't clobber a deliberate override.

✅ Closed at `rules.js:206`–`:213`, `derivations.js#defaultMobilityFor`, `AddShipDialog.svelte`.

#### M3: Supply caps were uniform; rulebook varies them by ship size/type

**Before (v1.0.1):** Every ship's supply tracks went up to a hardcoded `99` at the stepper.
**After (v1.0.2):** `SuppliesSection` reads `supplyCapsForShip(ship)` which prefers the canonical type's caps when one is selected (`SHIP_TYPE_PROFILES`), falling back to size-keyed defaults (`DEFAULT_SUPPLY_CAPS_BY_SIZE`) for free-text types. PDF source: page 173, Stores & Provisions table.

✅ Closed at `rules.js:272`–`:359`, `derivations.js#supplyCapsForShip`, `SuppliesSection.svelte`.

#### M4: The example fixture didn't match any canonical hull

**Before (v1.0.1):** `v1.example.shipsync.json` had non-canonical numbers (HP 30/30, gargantuan crew on a small frame, etc.).
**After (v1.0.2):** Rewritten as a canonical Sloop matching the page-198 profile end-to-end (HP 12/12, crew 24/12, supplies at Sloop caps, four-axis reputation, `Type: Sloop`).

✅ Closed at `src/lib/persistence/__fixtures__/v1.example.shipsync.json`.

## Out of Scope (explicit non-fixes)

- **Dice mechanics.** ShipSync intentionally doesn't roll. Anything that requires a die — damage rolls, attack-vs-AC, weather-gage contests, Mettle spends — stays at the table.
- **Phase-driven action gating.** The Movement / Attack / Status order is rendered as a label, not enforced; the captain can edit any field at any time.
- **Skeleton-crew action limit.** PDF p. 198 says a short-staffed ship can take only one of Movement / Attack / Status; ShipSync doesn't lock fields when crew dips below skeleton (would conflict with the "track, don't adjudicate" stance). The label and tone shift remain as the visible cue.
- **Heavy-weapons eligibility constraint.** `HEAVY_WEAPON_ELIGIBLE_TYPES` is data-only; the UI doesn't refuse to mount a heavy on a Sloop. Captain knows.
